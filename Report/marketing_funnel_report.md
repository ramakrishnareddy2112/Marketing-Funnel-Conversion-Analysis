# Marketing Funnel & Conversion Performance Analysis

## Project Overview

This project analyzes marketing funnel performance using the Bank Marketing Dataset. The objective is to identify conversion rates, funnel drop-offs, channel effectiveness, and customer segments that contribute to successful conversions.

## Dataset Information

* Total Records: 45,211
* Features: 17
* Target Variable: y (Customer Subscription)

## Funnel Performance

| Metric              | Value  |
| ------------------- | ------ |
| Total Leads         | 45,211 |
| Converted Customers | 5,289  |
| Conversion Rate     | 11.7%  |

## Key Findings

### 1. Funnel Drop-Off

A significant drop-off exists between total leads and converted customers. Only 11.7% of contacted customers subscribed.

### 2. Contact Channel Performance

Cellular contact methods achieved better conversion performance compared to other communication channels.

### 3. Job Category Analysis

Certain job categories showed higher conversion rates, indicating better customer responsiveness.

### 4. Monthly Trends

Conversion performance varied across months, highlighting seasonal marketing opportunities.

### 5. Education Impact

Customers with different educational backgrounds demonstrated varying conversion behaviors.

### 6. Housing Loan Impact

Housing loan status influenced customer subscription decisions.

## Recommendations

* Increase investment in high-performing communication channels.
* Focus marketing efforts on high-converting customer segments.
* Optimize campaigns during high-conversion periods.
* Personalize offers based on customer demographics.
* Improve follow-up strategies to reduce funnel drop-offs.

## Conclusion

The analysis reveals an overall conversion rate of 11.7%. By targeting high-performing channels and customer groups, organizations can improve conversion rates and optimize marketing expenditure.
